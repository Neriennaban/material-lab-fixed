# Steels Closure Notes

Source: `_OceanofPDF.com_Steels_-_H_K_D_H_Bhadeshia.pdf`

This note records the engineering extraction that is now materialized in the `pro` backend for Fe-C microstructure generation.

## Chapter-to-code mapping

- `Martensite in Steels`
  - `martensitic_family` remains a displacive family in `continuous transformation state`.
  - `lath_dominant / mixed_lath_plate / plate_dominant` is modeled as a morphology family, not a separate backend.
  - Koistinen-Marburger style undercooling remains the dominant progress driver for martensite.

- `Bainite in Steels`
  - Bainite is handled as a dedicated family with family-specific effective exposure and activation progress.
  - `upper_bainite_sheaves` and `lower_bainite_sheaves` are modeled as morphology splits only; they do not create new stage taxonomies.
  - Render/morphology distinctions are expressed through spacing, width bias, orientation variation, and feature masks.

- `Widmanstatten Ferrite`
  - Widmanstatten ferrite is represented as a ferrite morphology family selected from cooling/undercooling context.
  - It is spatially generated by needle/side-plate fields rather than by boundary allotriomorphic allocation.

- `Allotriomorphic Ferrite`
  - Allotriomorphic ferrite remains the default ferrite morphology family for slower or less undercooled ferritic formation.
  - It is expressed through boundary-biased spatial allocation and dedicated binary feature maps.

- `Pearlite`
  - Pearlite remains a lamellar colony family with spacing and colony scales tied to the continuous transformation state.
  - Family competition against ferrite is now mediated by direct family progress rather than by a standalone legacy heuristic.

- `Overall Transformation Kinetics`
  - Family-specific effective exposures are tracked for ferrite, pearlite, bainite, and martensite.
  - Family weights, progress metrics, and metadata propagation are driven by those exposures.

## Derived engineering labels

- `troostite_quench / sorbite_quench / troostite_temper / sorbite_temper` remain derived engineering labels over existing displacive or tempered families.
- They do not introduce separate physical generators.

## Closure criteria satisfied

- Family handoff is represented in `continuous transformation state`.
- Ferrite and bainite morphology splits are explicit in both metadata and morphology outputs.
- Stale tests tied to the old `competition_index` expectation were replaced by direct family-progress assertions.
- Metadata consistency and LR1-safe template application paths are covered by tests.
